# Task 0: Verify Classification

The email was pre-classified (see the keyword triage result in the email details). Before extracting data, VERIFY this classification is correct.

A true VEN-REM email is:
- FROM a vendor/supplier TO Planted Foods (not the other way around)
- About overdue or outstanding payments that Planted owes
- A REMINDER about previously issued invoices (not a new invoice being sent for the first time)

It is NOT VEN-REM if:
- VEN-INV: A new invoice being sent (no reminder language, no reference to overdue payment)
- CUST-REM-FOLLOWUP: A customer responding to a reminder PLANTED sent them (look for "Issued reminder")
- CUST-REMIT: A customer informing Planted of payments they made
- VEN-FOLLOWUP: Vendor is asking Planted for information, decisions, or a response (not just demanding payment).
  Includes: remittance advice requests, asking which invoices to net against a credit/Guthaben,
  payment breakdown queries, account statement confirmations.
  Key signal: vendor's email contains a question or request for Planted to specify/decide/confirm something.
- OTHER: None of the above

The email details section will include the keyword triage result. Consider the full context — keywords can match but the actual intent may differ.

In your output:

# Task 1: Determine Reminder Urgency Level

Analyze the language of the reminder (including email subject, body, and all attachments) and classify its urgency as level 1, 2, or 3.

**Level 1 (Standard):**
- First or simple payment reminder, mostly informative/polite
- No explicit or implied immediate risk to the company
- Typical wording: "1. Mahnung", "Zahlungserinnerung", simple "payment reminder"
- No mention of previous reminders, no clear threat of near-term harm
- No legal/collection language, no strong urgency beyond standard reminder phrasing

**Level 2 (High Priority):**
- Second, third, or explicitly "final" reminder
- Clearly urgent or repeated, near-term negative consequences likely
- May mention penalty fees or credit impact
- BUT no legal action, debt collection, or contract/relationship termination threatened
- Typical wording: "2. Mahnung", "3. Mahnung", "repeated reminder", "final notice"
- Warns about possible penalties if not paid soon

**Level 3 (Immediate Action Required):**
- Legal action threatened or already initiated
- Debt collection (Inkasso) involved or threatened
- Court proceedings (Betreibung, Mahnbescheid) mentioned
- Collection agency named or referenced
- Termination of business relationship or contract threatened ("Beendigung der Geschäftsbeziehung", "Kündigung")
- Service suspension, delivery hold, or supply stop threatened
- Explicit legal/enforcement language
- Typical wording: "Betreibungsandrohung", "Inkasso", "legal proceedings", "mise en demeure", "rechtliche Schritte", "Beendigung der Geschäftsbeziehung", "Lieferstopp"
